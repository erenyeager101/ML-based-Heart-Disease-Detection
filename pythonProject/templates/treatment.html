About.

Heart disease is a serious health condition that requires proper medical attention and treatment. The treatment options vary depending on the severity and type of heart disease.

Treatment

Heart disease treatment depends on the cause and type of heart damage. Healthy lifestyle habits — such as eating a low-fat, low-salt diet, getting regular exercise and good sleep, and not smoking — are an important part of treatment.

Some points are to be taken in consideration:

BP

High blood pressure

Eating a healthier diet with less salt, exercising regularly and taking medication can help lower blood pressure.

chest pain

Stents

Stents are small, expandable tubes that treat narrowed arteries in your body. In people with coronary heart disease caused by the buildup of plaque, they can: Open narrowed arteries Reduce symptoms, like chest pain Help treat a heart attack.

Pacemakers

A pacemaker is a small device that sends electrical impulses to the heart muscle to maintain a suitable heart rate and rhythm

The heart has its own pacemaker that regulates the rate at which it beats. But some hearts don't beat regularly, a problem called arrhythmia. Often, a pacemaker device can correct it.

Heart Attack Symptoms: What to Do in an Emergency.

Recognizing the Symptoms. Not all heart attacks begin with the sudden, crushing chest pain that most of us have heard about. In fact, some cause no symptoms at all, especially those that happen to people with diabetes. They may begin slowly, with mild pain and discomfort. They can happen while you're at rest or active. How severe they are can depend on your age, gender, and medical conditions.

  • Warning Signs
  • Chest discomfort that feels like pressure, fullness, or a squeezing pain
  • Pain and discomfort that go beyond your chest
  • Unexplained shortness of breath
  • with or without chest discomfort
  • Cold sweats Nausea or vomiting

  • What to Do When They Happen

    If you or someone you’re with has chest discomfort or other heart attack symptoms,While your first impulse may be to drive yourself or the heart attack victim to the hospital, it’s better to get an ambulance. Emergency medical services (EMS) personnel can start treatment on the way to the hospital. They’re also trained to revive a person if their heart stops.
  • Perform CPR if You Are Qualified
  • Cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) is one link in what the American Heart Association calls the "chain of survival." The chain of survival is a series of actions that, when done in sequence, will give a person having a heart attack the best chance of survival.
  • chain of survival is to do CPR until there’s access to a defibrillator.
  • The most common cause of death from a heart attack in adults is a disturbance in the electrical rhythm of the heart called ventricular fibrillation.
  • One way of buying time until a defibrillator becomes available is to give artificial breathing and circulation with CPR.
  • By giving a combination of manual chest compressions and artificial, or "mouth-to-mouth," respiration, the rescuer can breathe for the other person and help circulate some of the blood throughout their body.

  • CPR can be very effective.

    By doing CPR, you keep oxygenated blood flowing to the heart and brain until a defibrillator becomes available.
